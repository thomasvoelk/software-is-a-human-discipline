# No Absolutes

**Summary**: This philosophy describes what worked for me, not a prescription for everyone. I do not like to talk in absolutes.

**Sources**: Personal conviction.

**Last updated**: 2026-05-26

---

I do not like to talk in absolutes.

Everything in this wiki is something I have practised, tested, and found valuable — sometimes over many years. But I would never claim it works for everyone. People are different. Contexts are different. What became a habit for me through deliberate practice may not land the same way for someone else.

This matters when I mentor people. I share what I know and what worked for me. I do not hand down rules. The goal is to give someone a new thing to try, not a new thing to follow.

## Why this is worth stating explicitly

Software development culture is full of absolutes. Best practices. The right way. You should always. You should never. In my experience, most of those claims are someone's hard-won lesson from a specific context, overgeneralised.

I prefer: *this worked for me, here is why I think it did, try it if it resonates.*

## How to read this wiki

Every page here reflects my experience and my conclusions. Take what is useful. Leave what is not. If something resonates but does not work the way I describe it, that is fine — adapt it.

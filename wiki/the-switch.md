# The Switch

**Summary**: The pattern behind personal growth in this philosophy: awareness of a destructive state, then a deliberate flip to a constructive one. Requires serious practice before it becomes a new default.

**Sources**: Personal experience, Christopher Avery's Responsibility Process, J.B. Rainsberger (tweet).

**Last updated**: 2026-05-26

---

Two of the most powerful things I have learned have the same structure:

1. You notice you are in a bad state — defensive, angry, blaming, justifying.
2. You flip a switch.
3. You enter a better state — responsible, curious, open.

This sounds simple. It is not. For me, it took quite some practice before it became a habit. Things that used to feel threatening now feel interesting.

I call this "the switch" because that is how it feels — not a gradual shift, but a deliberate, almost physical flip.

## Two switches I use

**Switch 1: Responsibility** — when I catch myself in a powerless state (blaming, justifying, feeling shame), I flip to: *I create my own experience. What can I do?* See [[responsibility-process]].

**Switch 2: Curiosity** — when I feel anger or defensiveness rising (different opinion, criticism of my code, challenge to my design), I flip to: *I have never thought about this before. Tell me more.* See [[curiosity-over-anger]].

## Awareness + practice = a new default

The switch does not work on day one. Both of these required me months of deliberate practice before they became automatic. For me it went like this: catch yourself in the bad state, name it, flip. Over and over.
